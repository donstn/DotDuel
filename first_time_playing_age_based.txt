DotDuel — First-Time User Experience Report (age-banded)
==========================================================
Test date: 2026-05-17
Build: v22 · rhombus expanded to 36 dots
Target audience: Ages 5 through 50

Methodology
-----------
Simulated first-time user testing through five distinct age bands.
Each band was profiled, then walked through three play stages:
  1. First game ever — opens the app cold
  2. After 5-10 games — basic understanding
  3. After ~50 games — climbed difficulty / shape progression

Bands were chosen at points where behavior, vocabulary,
expectations, and physical interaction (finger size, vision)
shift meaningfully. They are NOT marketing segments.

Age bands (5 used):
  Band A: 5-7    pre-literate / early reader
  Band B: 8-11   literate, basic strategy
  Band C: 12-17  strategic, mobile-native
  Band D: 18-30  digital-native casual
  Band E: 31-50  casual / nostalgic

For each band: observations, direct quotes, blockers, lessons
learned over time, and what they'd ask for after 50 games.

The same game (build v22) was used for all bands. No tutorial
exists today, so observations include "what did they miss"
specifically because nothing was taught up front.

========================================================
BAND A — Ages 5-7
========================================================

Profile
-------
Pre-literate or early reader. Recognizes simple words ("Play",
"You") but full sentences in the Rules popover are inaccessible.
Counts confidently to 10, struggles past 20. Plays with a parent
or older sibling — hot-seat mode is the primary use case.
Finger taps are larger and less precise.

First game
----------
"I want the green balls."
"Why did it go dark? I wanted the lighter one."

The child taps Vs AI by accident, picks Triangle because it's
the only shape that isn't grey, then taps "Beginner" because
it's first. They don't know what "Beginner" means — they pick
left-to-right.

In-game the dots are immediately understood as "press a ball".
The pop animation gets giggling. They tap rapidly.

CONFUSION moments:
  - After the AI moves, the child taps "their" cream dot trying
    to "take it back". Cream dot doesn't react. Frustration.
  - When their move closes a line, the strike appears — they
    light up. When it closes TWO lines (one strikes, one doesn't
    because it's pending), the child cannot see the difference:
      "But I made it! Where's the line?"
    They tap the would-be line repeatedly, then a dot on it,
    which DOES claim. By accident they learn the mechanic.
  - Score numbers are abstract. "Am I winning?" "I don't know.
    Whose colour is bigger?"
  - Side panel avatars are LOVED. The L1 robot makes them laugh.

After 5-10 games
----------------
Mostly hot-seat with a parent. Has internalized "click dot makes
colour". Does NOT yet grasp lines as scoring units. Plays for
the satisfaction of filling dots, not for points.

The Beginner AI's pure-random moves feel like a "friend" — the
child often wins, which keeps them coming back. No frustration
with Beginner.

Easy unlocks. Child picks it because it's "next". Easy AI
suddenly grabs corners. Child loses. Confusion:
  "Why is it cheating?"

Returns to Beginner happily.

After ~50 games
---------------
Still 90% Beginner level. Has stopped touching the difficulty
list. Plays Triangle exclusively (Square is "too big"). Cannot
read "Impossible" so it's not aspirational.

Notices "pending" lines after dozens of plays — finally connects
that tapping coloured balls counts. Doesn't articulate the rule,
but uses it.

Specific to this band — top three blockers
------------------------------------------
  1. The Rules popover is wholly inaccessible (text-heavy).
  2. "Pending" lines have ZERO visual cue. The child literally
     cannot see the rule they need to learn. Discovered only by
     accidental dot-tapping.
  3. Numbers and English labels (Beginner, Easy, etc.) are
     opaque. Difficulty selection is left-to-right.

Quote of the band
-----------------
  "Mama, why does the robot win sometimes? I made the line!"

========================================================
BAND B — Ages 8-11
========================================================

Profile
-------
Reads short sentences confidently. Counts to 100. Can grasp
basic strategy ("place where it scores"). Mobile-native; thumb
taps precise. Will read the Rules popover ONCE if the first game
is confusing — otherwise skims and moves on.

First game
----------
Opens app. Recognises "DotDuel" as a game name. Sees the green
glass title and "v22 · rhombus expanded to 36 dots" badge.
Ignores the version badge.

Picks Vs AI > Triangle > Beginner.

In-game: places dots, sees the AI's cream dots. Notices the
strike appear after their move. "Cool. So that's the point."

Closes the first multi-line move. ONE strike draws. Pauses.
  "Hold on. That should be two."

Opens the Rules popover (notices the "?" icon in topbar).
Reads "The catch — one move, one score". Closes popover.

  "Oh. Sneaky."

The child IMMEDIATELY understands the pending mechanic from the
written explanation — but doesn't yet know how to claim. Returns
to game. Sees nothing visually marked as "pending". Closes app.

After 5-10 games
----------------
Returns. Tries claiming by tapping ON the would-be line. Nothing
happens (line click area is gone — only dots claim). Eventually
taps a dot belonging to a pending line — strike appears.

  "OH. You tap the BALL, not the line. That's weird."

Now using both place + claim moves. Beats Beginner consistently.
Unlocks Easy. Beats Easy after a few losses. Unlocks Square.

Easy unlock triggers the "Easy unlocked!" CTA on game-over.
Child taps it directly. Skips menu. Loves the flow.

After ~50 games
---------------
Working through Triangle Medium. Has cleared Triangle Easy on
Square. Discovered Hot-seat with a sibling — favourite mode now.

Comments about UI:
  - Loves the glass aesthetic ("looks like ice")
  - Wishes for sound effects
  - Doesn't understand "rating" placeholder slot — asks "what's the
    dash mean?". Once told it's for online, accepts it.
  - Active-player glow border subtle — they sometimes lose track
    of whose turn it is and tap when AI is thinking. Tap registers
    nothing (correctly blocked) but no feedback that "it's not
    your turn yet".

Specific blockers
-----------------
  1. Claim-by-tap on a dot is non-obvious. They expected to tap
     the line. (Universal blocker, but this band figures it out
     within 3-5 games.)
  2. "Whose turn?" — active panel glow is too subtle. They want
     explicit text or a brighter halo.
  3. AI "thinking" delay (450 ms) is fine the first time but
     feels slow on game 20+.

Quote of the band
-----------------
  "Wait — you have to TAP THE BALL not the line. That's the
  whole game right there."

========================================================
BAND C — Ages 12-17
========================================================

Profile
-------
Mobile-native, fast learner, low patience for tutorials.
Skips Rules popover entirely on first open. Strong strategic
brain. Wants to win fast and climb to Impossible.

First game
----------
Opens app. Skips everything. Hammers Vs AI > Triangle > Beginner.
"Easy, this is dot game."

Beats Beginner without reading rules. Wins by random luck. Sees
"Easy unlocked, try Easy" CTA. Taps it.

Loses Easy. Annoyed.
  "I scored more! Why did the AI win?"

Re-checks the score. They DID score more — they're remembering
wrong. AI scored a bigger final line. They didn't see it because
it was a pending claim by AI.

Opens Rules popover (finally). Reads the highlight section
about "one move, one score". Closes it.

  "Wait that's actually clever."

After 5-10 games
----------------
Now playing strategically. Avoids multi-line completions. Notices
that the AI sometimes "leaves" pending lines for them.

Climbs through Easy → Medium → Hard on Triangle. Hard is a real
fight. Cleared after several attempts.

Tries Impossible. Loses badly first 3 games. Score gap small but
loses on close-game tiebreak.
  "Yo this AI is actually nuts."

Refreshes the page expecting a "rematch" button — finds it on the
game-over screen ("Play again"). Fine.

After ~50 games
---------------
Beat Triangle Impossible. Sees the "Impossible — defeated" gold
title and the suggestion to try Square. Taps "Try Square". Loves
the flow.

Works through Square, Rectangle, Rhombus. Beats Rhombus Impossible.
Sees "DotDuel champion" gold title. Reads suggestion: "Multiplayer
coming soon". DISAPPOINTED.

  "Wait, that's it? No leaderboard? Where do my friends play?"

Comments about UI:
  - Wants leaderboard or rating to actually work (it shows '—'
    in side panel — feels like a TODO)
  - 450 ms AI delay too slow for late-game grinding — wants a
    fast mode
  - Multiplayer absence is the single biggest letdown after
    completing single-player
  - Aesthetic is great. Glass orbs, gold strike on champion screen
    — "polish like a real game"

Specific blockers
-----------------
  1. Rating slot shows "—" — they want it to mean something.
  2. AI delay too long for repeat plays.
  3. No multiplayer is a hard stop — they have nothing left after
     finishing single-player.

Quote of the band
-----------------
  "Where's the online mode?"

========================================================
BAND D — Ages 18-30
========================================================

Profile
-------
Adult digital native. Plays casually on phone (commute, lunch
break). Compares to similar games (Dots & Boxes, Threes!,
2048). Reads rules quickly. Appreciates polish.

First game
----------
Opens app, reads tagline ("Take turns. Complete lines. Get the
most points."). Briefly scans Rules popover. Got the gist.

  "OK, it's Dots & Boxes with a twist."

Picks Vs AI > Triangle > Beginner. Plays one game to feel the
mechanic. Notices the pending claim immediately because the
rules said so. Tries tapping the strike line — doesn't work.
Tries tapping a coloured ball — works. Files away.

  "Tap-the-dot to claim is awkward. Took me three tries."

After 5-10 games
----------------
Climbs through Beginner → Medium fast. Hard takes a few rounds.
Notices balance: "this feels actually fair, the AI doesn't
cheat".

Reads version badge ("v22 · rhombus expanded to 36 dots"). Reacts
positively to active development feel.

Plays Hot-seat once with a friend. Friend (also adult) gets
confused at first move, asks "wait, what just happened" when
the multi-line catch fires. Explains, friend gets it instantly.

After ~50 games
---------------
Has cleared at least Hard on all four shapes. Working on
Impossible. Comments:

  - Glass aesthetic is excellent. Looks like a paid app.
  - Triangle expansion (36 dots) feels better-paced than
    smaller boards would.
  - 450 ms AI delay is fine. Doesn't bug them.
  - Wants statistics — total games played, win streaks, time
    spent. Power-user instinct.
  - Wants account / cross-device sync. localStorage erasing
    on browser-clear is concerning.
  - Multiplayer "coming soon" — patient about it, but it's
    where they'd spend most of their playtime.
  - Footer says "Privacy" and "Contact" — both don't do
    anything. Concerning for a public app.

Specific blockers
-----------------
  1. Privacy / Contact links broken — looks unprofessional.
  2. Footer Rating slot ('—') and stub links signal "not finished".
  3. No multiplayer, no leaderboard — limits replay value
     after climbing the unlock chain.
  4. No statistics tracking — adult casuals want completion %
     and win records.

Quote of the band
-----------------
  "It looks polished, but the Privacy link goes nowhere. That
  immediately worries me about my data."

========================================================
BAND E — Ages 31-50
========================================================

Profile
-------
Adult casual. May have kids of their own. Memory of similar
paper-and-pen games (the user explicitly noted playing with
their mother from age 5 — this is the band that connects to
that nostalgia). Patience varies. Mobile fluency varies — some
are sub-par on tiny touch targets and small fonts.

First game
----------
Opens app on phone. First thing noticed: text. Small. Squints.

  "OK what is this. Oh, dots on paper. We used to play this."

Reads Rules popover word-for-word. Takes ~60 seconds. Closes it.
Feels prepared. Picks Vs AI > Triangle > Beginner.

In-game: makes a placement. Pop animation appreciated. AI moves.
Player notices the AI uses light cream — wonders momentarily if
THEY are the light cream (no — they're the dark green, since
they went first and the colour matches their first dot). Side
panel confirms "You" is on the left with the human avatar.

  "OK I'm on the left. Got it."

Closes one multi-line. Sees only one strike.
  "Hmm. Thought that should be two lines."

Recalls the rules. Tries to claim by tapping the would-be line.
Nothing happens. Tries tapping the dots — works.
  "Oh, the rule said tap the dot. Yeah."

Slow first game but completes it.

After 5-10 games
----------------
Comfortable with the loop. Plays at a relaxed pace. Notices
small UX nags:

  - Active-player highlight subtle. They lose track of whose
    turn after looking away mid-game.
  - "thinking…" pulse dots on AI panel are clear but tiny.
  - Score numbers in side panel are readable, but the rating
    placeholder ("—") confuses. "Is something supposed to be
    there?"
  - Game-over CTAs are great. The direct "Try Easy" button
    means they don't have to navigate back. Appreciated.

Wins Beginner. Tries Easy. Loses several times. Realizes Easy
takes free corners aggressively. Adapts.

Discovers Hot-seat. Plays with kid (age 8). Kid loves the
robots. Adult enjoys explaining strategy. Hot-seat is the
primary mode for this player.

After ~50 games
---------------
Mostly hot-seat with the kid. Vs AI is for solo commute time —
they prefer Medium difficulty as a "thinking game" without too
much frustration.

Comments:

  - "When can I play with my friend online?" — multiplayer
    expectation.
  - "I love the robot avatars. The L1 one looks like one of
    my kid's toys."
  - Hot-seat with kid: would love a way to swap which colour
    each player is (no current way). The 5-year-old always
    wants the cream colour but the system always assigns it
    to whoever plays second.
  - Small touch targets occasionally — when they're tired or
    on a smaller phone, they double-tap accidentally.
  - Footer text small. They read it once. After that, ignore.

Specific blockers
-----------------
  1. Colour assignment is fixed by turn order. Player 1 = dark,
     Player 2 = cream. Kids fight over this in Hot-seat.
  2. Active-player glow too subtle for "look away then back"
     casual play.
  3. Touch targets borderline-small on smaller phones — the
     larger dots in Triangle/Rhombus are fine, but small Square
     dots are tight.
  4. Footer "Privacy" and "Contact" don't work — same as Band D.

Quote of the band
-----------------
  "My daughter and I play this on the couch. She's five. The
  little smiling robot makes her happy. But she always wants
  the white balls and the game won't let her have them."

========================================================
UNIVERSAL FINDINGS (across all bands)
========================================================

#1 — Pending claim mechanic is the single biggest UX gap
---------------------------------------------------------
The "biggest line scores, others go pending" rule is the most
unique mechanic in DotDuel. Today there is ZERO visual signal
for pending lines. Each age band rediscovers the mechanic by
accident (Band A) or through the rules (Bands B-E). Once they
get it, they like it — but the discovery cost is steep.

Across all 5 bands, the average "time-to-first-claim" was
~6 games. That is too long. Recommended fixes:

  - First-time user only: subtle pulse on a dot belonging to
    a pending line, fading away after they make their first
    claim. Once they've claimed once, the hint disappears.
  - OR: brief floating tooltip after the first multi-line
    completion explaining "tap a coloured ball to claim".
  - OR: a "show hints" toggle in settings (off by default for
    expert UX, on by default for first 10 games).

#2 — Active player indicator too subtle
-----------------------------------------
Bands B, C, E all flagged that the colored glow around the active
panel doesn't grab attention when looking away. Recommended fix:
add a tiny breathing animation (slow opacity pulse on the panel
border) when it's your turn AND it's been more than ~3 seconds
since the last action. Don't pulse constantly — only after a
visual pause.

#3 — Footer stub links erode trust
----------------------------------
Privacy / Contact links in the footer don't do anything (return
preventDefault). Adult bands (D, E) noticed and lost trust. Either
remove the stub links until they work, or wire them to dummy
content pages explaining the project is in development.

#4 — Color/role assignment is fixed
------------------------------------
In Hot-seat mode there is no way to pick which colour you are.
Band E specifically called out: kid wants cream, system assigns
dark green. Recommended fix: a swap button in the menu before
starting Hot-seat.

#5 — Audio is absent
--------------------
All bands either noticed the silence or weren't bothered. Band B
(kids) explicitly wanted "boop" sounds on dot placement and a
victory jingle. Adult bands didn't notice or appreciate the
silence (good for commute). Recommended: subtle SFX with a clear
master mute toggle.

#6 — Statistics are absent
---------------------------
Bands C and D want win records, streaks, time spent. Current
localStorage only tracks unlocks. Recommended: add a small
statistics block (in menu or game-over) showing total games,
wins, draws.

#7 — "Rating" placeholder confuses non-tech users
--------------------------------------------------
The "—" rating slot in side panels reads as "broken" to Bands A
and E. Either hide it until multiplayer ships, or show "Local"
or "Offline" as the placeholder text.

#8 — No tutorial overlay on first launch
-----------------------------------------
Every band would have benefited from a 3-step overlay:
  1. "Tap a dot to colour it."
  2. "Fill a line to score its length."
  3. "If your move completes two lines, only the longest
     scores. Tap a coloured ball later to claim the rest."

Skippable, shown once.

========================================================
AGE-BAND PRIORITY RANKING — top 3 fixes per band
========================================================

Band A (5-7):
  1. Tutorial overlay (no-text version, animations only)
  2. Pending-line visual hint (first-time toggle)
  3. Larger touch targets / louder feedback

Band B (8-11):
  1. Active-turn breathing animation
  2. Pending-line hint (first 10 games)
  3. Sound effects toggle

Band C (12-17):
  1. Multiplayer
  2. Fast-mode toggle (reduce AI delay)
  3. Functional rating slot or hide it

Band D (18-30):
  1. Functional Privacy / Contact links
  2. Statistics tracking
  3. Cross-device sync (Google auth)

Band E (31-50):
  1. Hot-seat colour-swap (huge for parent + kid play)
  2. Brighter active-player indicator
  3. Functional footer links

========================================================
TWO BANDS WHERE THE LINE IS SHARP
========================================================

The most pronounced age boundary is between Band A and Band B:
literacy. Below 8, the Rules popover is non-existent, the
"Impossible" label is opaque, and the entire UX is icon-driven
+ caregiver-mediated. Above 8, they can self-learn and self-
correct.

The second-sharpest boundary is between Band C and Band D:
patience for missing features. Teens loudly want multiplayer
and call out the absence; adults patient-shrug and play what's
there.

========================================================
WHAT IS WORKING WELL (don't break these)
========================================================

  - Glass aesthetic is universally praised. Premium feel.
  - Robot avatars (L1-L5) — especially L1 — are charming.
    Loved by kids, appreciated by adults.
  - The "Try Square" / "Try Hard" direct CTAs on game-over
    are excellent. Every band praised them.
  - Side-panel layout (avatar/name/rating/score) is clean and
    readable.
  - Difficulty progression and unlock chain creates a clear
    "campaign" feeling.
  - The "DotDuel champion" gold styling for full completion is
    a strong emotional payoff.
  - Hot-seat mode is appreciated by Bands A and E — the
    multi-generational play is a real use case.

========================================================
SUGGESTED PRIORITY ORDER FOR NEXT SPRINT
========================================================

P0 — must fix before public launch
  1. Wire or hide Privacy / Contact stub links.
  2. Add a first-time-user tutorial overlay (skippable).

P1 — improve discoverability
  3. Pending-line subtle hint for first ~10 games.
  4. Brighter active-player indicator (breathing pulse after pause).
  5. Hot-seat colour-swap button.

P2 — depth & retention
  6. Sound effects with master mute.
  7. Statistics tracking (local).
  8. Fast-mode toggle (configurable AI delay).

P3 — feature work (separately planned)
  9. Multiplayer with Google auth (currently in roadmap).
 10. Cross-device sync.

========================================================
END OF REPORT
========================================================

Notes on methodology limitations:
  - This is simulated user testing, not real observation. Numbers
    like "average time-to-first-claim ~6 games" are educated guesses
    based on the mechanic's invisibility, not measured.
  - Each band represents a composite user, not a single person.
  - No A/B testing was done — recommendations are based on
    interaction-design heuristics and prior research in casual game
    UX (e.g., the standard "tutorial overlay" pattern dominant in
    mobile games since ~2014).
  - Real user testing with at least 3 participants per band would
    be the next validation step before shipping P0/P1 fixes.
